## 🔍 Topic-Wise Breakdown

## 📌 4.1 Basic Formulae

| Concept | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Fraction to Percent | Multiply by 100 and add `%`. Example: $ \frac{3}{5} = 60\% $ |
| Percent to Fraction | Divide by 100. Example: $ 16\frac{2}{3}\% = \frac{1}{6} $ |
| Finding a Percentage of a Number | Use formula: $ \frac{x}{100} \times N $. Example: $ 75\% \text{ of } 400 = 300 $ |

## 📌 4.2 Short-Cut Methods

| Method | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| A is x% more than B → B is less than A by | $ \frac{x}{100 + x} \times 100\% $ |
| A is x% less than B → B is more than A by | $ \frac{x}{100 - x} \times 100\% $ |
| A is x% of C and B is y% of C → A is | $ \frac{x}{y} \times 100\% $ of B |
| Two Numbers More/Less Than Third | Use ratios: $ \frac{100 + x}{100 + y} \times 100\% $ |
| Price Change & Consumption | $ \frac{P}{100 + P} \times 100\% $ (increase), $ \frac{P}{100 - P} \times 100\% $ (decrease) |
| Successive Percentage Change | $ x + y + \frac{xy}{100} \% $ |
| Population Growth / Decay | $ P \left(1 + \frac{r}{100}\right)^n $ |
| Combined Changes in Product | $ x + y + \frac{xy}{100} \% $ |

## 📊 Summary Table: Key Formulas

| Type | Formula | Description |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Fraction to Percent | $ \frac{a}{b} \rightarrow \frac{a}{b} \times 100\% $ | Convert any fraction to percent |
| Percent to Fraction | $ x\% \rightarrow \frac{x}{100} $ | Convert percent to fraction |
| x% of N | $ \frac{x}{100} \times N $ | Find percentage of a number |
| A is x% more than B | $ \frac{x}{100 + x} \times 100\% $ | B is less than A by |
| A is x% less than B | $ \frac{x}{100 - x} \times 100\% $ | B is more than A by |
| Successive Percentage | $ x + y + \frac{xy}{100} \% $ | Overall change after multiple changes |

## 📋 Examples and Applications

## ✅ Example: Successive Percentage Change

If the price of a product increases by 15% and then decreases by 12%, calculate the overall change.

$$
\text{Net change} = 15 - 12 - \frac{15 \times 12}{100} = 1.2\%
$$

## ✅ Example: Area of Square with Side Increased by 20%

If the side of a square is increased by 20%, calculate the percentage increase in area.

$$
\text{New area} = (\text{side})^2 \Rightarrow \text{Change} = 20 + 20 + \frac{20 \times 20}{100} = 44\%
$$

## ✅ Example: Revenue Effect Due to Tax and Consumption Change

If tax decreases by 15% and consumption increases by 10%, calculate the effect on revenue.

$$
\text{Net change} = -15 + 10 + \frac{-15 \times 10}{100} = -6.5\% \Rightarrow \text{Revenue decreases by 6.5\%}
$$
