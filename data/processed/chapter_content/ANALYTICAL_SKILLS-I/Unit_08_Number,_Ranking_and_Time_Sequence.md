## **1. Number Test**

## **Key Concepts**
- Identify specific numbers in sequences based on given conditions.
- Numbers may appear in patterns, and you need to identify their positions or occurrences.
- Conditions often involve:
  - Preceding or following another number.
  - Being in a specific position within the sequence.
  - Following a rule like "not immediately followed by X".

## **Examples and Problem Types**
- Counting numbers with specific properties.
- Reversing digits within numbers and sorting them.
- Identifying patterns between digits.

## **Example**
> **Question:** How many 5s are there in the following number sequence each of which is immediately preceded by 3 or 4 but not immediately followed by 8 or 9?

> **Sequence:** `3 5 9 5 4 5 5 3 5 8 4 5 6 7 3 5 7 5 5 4 5 2 3 5 1 0`

> **Answer:** 5

## **Practice Tip**
- Focus on identifying positional relationships.
- Look for clues about preceding/following numbers.
- Practice reversing and sorting numbers.

## **2. Ranking Test**

## **Key Concepts**
- Determine the **total number of people** in a group using **ranks from top/bottom**.
- Understand formulas:
  - $ \text{Total} = (\text{Top Rank} + \text{Bottom Rank}) - 1 $
  - $ \text{Position from Opposite Side} = (\text{Total} - \text{Given Side Position}) + 1 $

## **Examples and Problem Types**
- Given rank from top and bottom, calculate total participants.
- Interchange seating arrangements or positions.
- Use logical reasoning to derive missing information.

## **Example**
> **Question:** Rohan ranks 7th from the top and 26th from the bottom in a class. How many students are in the class?

> **Answer:** 32

## **Practice Tip**
- Always check if the problem involves overlapping positions or interchanges.
- Be careful not to double count the individual being ranked.
- Apply formulas directly once you understand the logic behind them.

## **3. Time Sequence Test**

## **Key Concepts**
- Determine **dates**, **days**, or **events** based on relative positioning.
- Often used to resolve **conflicting statements** about events occurring on specific dates.
- Requires understanding of **relative time intervals**.

## **Examples and Problem Types**
- Finding the **correct date** based on multiple constraints.
- Calculating **days ahead or behind** a reference point.
- Solving puzzles involving **time gaps** and **sequence overlaps**.

## **Example**
> **Question:** Satish remembers that his brother's birthday is after 15th but before 18th of February. His sister remembers it is after 16th but before 19th. What is the correct date?

> **Answer:** 17th February

## **Practice Tip**
- List out possible values based on each constraint.
- Find the intersection of all possible outcomes.
- Visualize timelines or use calendars for clarity.

## Review & Practice Questions

1. **Family Relationship**: Radha says "His father is the only son of my grandfather". So, Radha is Ajay's **mother**.
2. **Counting 4's**: Count how many 4's are preceded by 7 but not followed by 3.
3. **Class Rank**: Rohan is 7th from top and 26th from bottom → Total students = 7 + 26 - 1 = **32**.
4. **Position from Left**: Manik is 14th from right in a row of 40 → Position from left = 40 - 14 + 1 = **27th**.
5. **Row of Boys**: A is 16th from left, C is 16th from right, B is 4th to the right of A and 5th to the left of C → Total boys = 16 + 16 - 1 = **31**.
6. **Pattern Recognition**: Count how many times 1, 3, and 7 appear together with 7 in the middle. Answer: **4 times**.
7. **Descending Order**: Numbers divisible by 5 from 5 to 85 → 11th from bottom = **70**.
8. **Position Swap**: Anil moves from 7th to 21st → Total students = 21 + 18 - 1 = **38**.
9. **Tallest/Shortest**: Shortest is **Rajat**.
10. **Departure Time**: Leaves home at 6:40 am, arrives at 7:05 am, leaves for office at 7:20 am → Departure from Vineeta's house = **7:05 am**.
