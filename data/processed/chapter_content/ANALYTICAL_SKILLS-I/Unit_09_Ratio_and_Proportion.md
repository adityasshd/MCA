## **Study Guide: Unit 09 - Ratio and Proportion**

## **Overview**
This study guide covers essential topics related to **Ratio** and **Proportion**, along with applications involving **Percentages**, **Equivalent Ratios**, and solving real-world problems using **Cross-Multiplication** techniques.

## **Key Concepts**

## **1. Understanding Ratios**
- A **ratio** compares two quantities by division.
- Written as $ a:b $ or $ \frac{a}{b} $.
- The first quantity is the **antecedent**, the second is the **consequent**.
- Both quantities must be in the **same unit**.

## Example:
If a bag weighs 45 kg and another weighs 29 kg, the ratio is $ 45:29 $.

## **2. Proportions**
- A **proportion** is an equation stating that two ratios are equal.
- Notation: $ a:b = c:d $ or $ a:b::c:d $
- **Cross-multiplication Rule**: If $ a:b = c:d $, then $ a \cdot d = b \cdot c $

## Example:
$ 3:4 = 6:8 $ → $ 3 \cdot 8 = 4 \cdot 6 $

## **3. Equivalent Ratios**
- Ratios are equivalent if their simplified forms are equal.
- Use cross-multiplication to check equivalence.

## Example:
Check if $ \frac{2}{5} = \frac{4}{10} $ → $ 2 \cdot 10 = 5 \cdot 4 $ → 20 = 20 ✔️

## **4. Types of Proportions**
| Term | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Means** | The middle terms in a proportion $ a:b = c:d $, i.e., $ b $ and $ c $ |
| **Extremes** | The outermost terms in a proportion $ a:b = c:d $, i.e., $ a $ and $ d $ |
| **Product of Means = Product of Extremes** | $ b \cdot c = a \cdot d $ |

## **5. Special Cases in Proportions**
| Concept | Formula | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| **Fourth Proportional** | $ \frac{b \cdot c}{a} $ | Given $ a:b = c:x $, find $ x $ |
| **Third Proportional** | $ \frac{b^2}{a} $ | Given $ a:b = b:x $, find $ x $ |
| **Mean Proportional** | $ \sqrt{ab} $ | Given $ a:x = x:b $, find $ x $ |

## **6. Application of Ratios and Proportions**
| Problem Type | Strategy |
|--------------|----------|
| Finding missing value in a proportion | Set up a proportion and use cross-multiplication |
| Mixing two substances in a ratio | Use the formula: $ \frac{a}{b} = \frac{x}{y} $, where $ x $ and $ y $ are parts of the mixture |
| Percentage problems | Convert percentage to fraction: $ \% = \frac{\text{part}}{\text{whole}} \times 100 $ |

## **Important Formulas**
| Formula | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Finding individual values from total** | If two numbers are in ratio $ a:b $, and their sum is $ x $, then:<br>First number = $ \frac{a}{a+b} \cdot x $<br>Second number = $ \frac{b}{a+b} \cdot x $ |
| **Finding missing value in a chain of ratios** | Combine ratios by aligning common terms:<br>$ a:b = c:d $, $ b:e = f:g $, etc. |
| **Finding missing value after addition/subtraction** | Solve using linear equations derived from new ratio expressions |

## **Examples & Solutions**

## Example 1: Solving a Proportion
$$
\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}
$$
→ Cross multiply: $ a \cdot d = b \cdot c $

## Example 2: Finding Fourth Proportional
Given $ 2:5 = 4:x $, find $ x $:
$$
\frac{2}{5} = \frac{4}{x} \Rightarrow x = \frac{5 \cdot 4}{2} = 10
$$

## Example 3: Finding Mean Proportional
Find the mean proportional between 48 and 12:
$$
x = \sqrt{48 \cdot 12} = \sqrt{576} = 24
$$

## Example 4: Real-World Application
A train travels at 68 mph for 3.5 hours. How far does it go?
$$
\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \cdot \text{Time} = 68 \cdot 3.5 = 238 \text{ miles}
$$

## **Practice Problems**

| Problem | Solution Method |
|--------|------------------|
| Given $ a:b = 3:4 $, find $ a $ if $ a + b = 21 $. | Use $ a = \frac{3}{3+4} \cdot 21 $ |
| Find the third proportional to 2.5 and 1.5. | Use $ x = \frac{b^2}{a} = \frac{1.5^2}{2.5} = 0.9 $ |
| If $ a:b = 4:5 $ and $ a + b = 27 $, find $ a $ and $ b $. | $ a = \frac{4}{9} \cdot 27 = 12 $, $ b = 15 $ |

## **1. Introduction to Ratios**

## **What is a Ratio?**
A ratio compares two quantities of the same kind. It expresses the relationship between the sizes of two numbers.

## **Notation**
- A ratio of a to b is written as `a:b`.

## **Examples**
- The ratio of boys to girls in a class is 3:2.

## **Key Concepts**
- Ratios can be simplified like fractions.
- Ratios can be compared using cross-multiplication.

## **Formula**
$$ \frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d} $$

## **2. Proportion**

## **Definition**
When two ratios are equal, they form a proportion.

## **Properties of Proportion**
- **Cross Product Rule**: If $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}$, then $ad = bc$.
- **Continued Proportion**: If $a:b = b:c$, then $b^2 = ac$.

## **Types of Proportions**
- Direct Proportion
- Indirect/Inverse Proportion

## **3. Solving Problems with Ratios**

## **Finding the Number to Be Subtracted or Added**
To find the number to be subtracted or added to change the ratio:

## **Subtraction Case**
$$ x = \frac{(bc - ad)}{(c - d)} $$

## **Addition Case**
$$ x = \frac{(bc - ad)}{(a + d - b - c)} $$

**Example:**  
Find the number to be subtracted from the terms of the ratio 5:6 to make it equal to 2:3.
$$ x = \frac{(6 \cdot 2 - 5 \cdot 3)}{(3 - 2)} = \frac{(12 - 15)}{1} = 3 $$

## **4. Mixtures and Alligation**

## **Alligation Method**
Used to determine the ratio in which two components should be mixed to obtain a desired mixture.

## **Formula**
$$ \text{Required ratio} = \frac{\text{Difference of the higher price}}{\text{Difference of the lower price}} $$

## **Example**
In what ratio should tea costing ₹9/kg and ₹15/kg be mixed to get a mixture costing ₹10.2/kg?
$$ \text{Ratio} = \frac{15 - 10.2}{10.2 - 9} = \frac{4.8}{1.2} = 4:1 $$

## **5. Cost Price and Mixture Problems**

## **Cost Price Calculation**
If two items of different prices are mixed, the cost price of the mixture is calculated as:

$$ \text{Cost Price of Mixture} = \frac{q_1 c_1 + q_2 c_2}{q_1 + q_2} $$

Where:
- $q_1$: quantity of item 1
- $c_1$: cost of item 1
- $q_2$: quantity of item 2
- $c_2$: cost of item 2
