## **2. Introduction**

Data can be presented in various forms such as:

- **Line Graphs**: Used to show changes in data over equal intervals of time.
- **Bar Diagrams**: Ideal for comparing categorical data.
- **Tables**: Useful for detailed data organization.
- **Pie Charts**: Effective for showing the composition of a whole.

**Key Concepts:**
- Line graphs help in analyzing trends like profit increases, growth rates, and capacity utilization.
- Capacity utilization is calculated as:  
  $$
  \text{Capacity Utilization} = \left(\frac{\text{Actual Output}}{\text{Maximum Capacity}}\right) \times 100
  $$

## **3. Key Topics Explained**

## **3.1 Bar Diagram**

A **bar diagram** represents data using rectangular bars, where the length of the bar corresponds to the value of the entity being measured.

## Types of Bar Diagrams:
| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Column Bar Chart | Vertical bars |
| Row Bar Chart | Horizontal bars |

## Uses:
- Compare categorical data
- Display discrete data
- Present multiple datasets simultaneously (multiple bar diagrams)

## Example:
- **Multiple Bar Diagram**: Compares sales across different regions.
- **Percentage Bar Diagram**: Shows the proportion of components within a category.

## **3.2 Cubes and Dices**

## Cube Properties:
- **Vertices**: 8
- **Faces**: 6 (all equal)
- **Edges**: 12 (all equal)

## Formulae for Painted Cubes:
- **Only One Face Painted**: $ (n-2)^2 \times 6 $
- **Exactly Two Faces Painted**: $ (n-2) \times 12 $
- **Exactly Three Faces Painted**: $ (n-2)^3 $

## **3.3 Dices**

- **Symmetric Dice**: Opposite faces add up to 7 (1-6, 2-5, 3-4).
- **Asymmetric Dice**: Opposite faces do not add up to the same value.

## **9. Notes on Examples**

## Example 1: Capacity Utilization
- Given: Actual Output = 120 cars/day; Maximum Capacity = 150 cars/day
- Calculation: $ \frac{120}{150} \times 100 = 80\% $

## Example 2: Profit Increase
- Given: Profit in 2001–02 = Rs. 100 crores; Profit in 2003–04 = Rs. 104.16 crores
- Calculation: $ \frac{104.16 - 100}{100} \times 100 = 4.16\% $

## Example 3: Consumption Comparison
- Given: Metals vs Plastics consumption for 2006–2011
- Conclusion: Metals consumed more than plastics in certain years

## Example 4: Income vs Expenditure
- Given: Income in 1998 = 264 crores; Profit = 65%
- Calculation: Expenditure = $ \frac{264}{1.65} = 160 $ crores
