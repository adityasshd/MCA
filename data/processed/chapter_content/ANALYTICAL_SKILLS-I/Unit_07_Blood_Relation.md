## Unit 07: Blood Relation Study Guide

## 📚 Overview

This study guide provides a structured overview of **Blood Relations**, a core topic in **Logical Reasoning**. It covers all types of questions typically encountered in competitive exams and includes definitions, examples, and problem-solving strategies.

## 🔍 Objective

After completing this unit, learners will be able to:

- Understand how to determine **Family or Blood Relationships**.
- Identify relationships involving the term **"-in-law"**.
- Differentiate between **Father-in-law**, **Mother-in-law**, **Son-in-law**, and **Daughter-in-law**.
- Recognize **Brother-in-law** and **Sister-in-law** relationships.
- Distinguish between **Half Siblings** and **Step Relations**.

## 🧠 Introduction

**Blood Relations** refer to connections between individuals by **birth** or **marriage**. These relationships are crucial in logical reasoning and frequently appear in competitive exams due to their complexity and diversity.

## 📘 7.1 Types of Questions on Blood Relation

| Type                        | Description |
|----------------------------|-------------|
| **Dialogue-Based**         | Based on conversations where a person describes their relation to someone else. |
| **Puzzle-Based**           | Requires forming a network of relationships based on given information. |
| **Coding-Decoding**        | Uses symbols or codes to denote relationships. |

## ✅ Tips for Solving:
- Do **not assume** genders from names.
- Use **diagrams** to visualize relationships.
- Be careful with **complex puzzles**.
- Use **pictorial methods** for coding-decoding problems.

## 🗣️ 7.2 Family or Blood Relations Test

## Steps to Solve:
1. **Identify the two persons** involved in the relationship.
2. **Pinpoint the intermediate relationships** connecting them.
3. **Conclude the direct relationship** between the two persons.

## Symbols Used:
- Male: `⊕`
- Female: `□`

## 📌 Basic Terms in Blood Relations

| Term       | Definition                                                                 |
|------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Parents    | Mother and Father                                                         |
| Children   | Son or Daughter                                                           |
| Siblings   | Brother or Sister (of the same parents)                                  |
| Spouse     | Husband or Wife                                                          |
| Aunt       | Father’s sister, Mother’s sister, Father’s brother’s wife, or Mother’s brother’s wife |
| Uncle      | Father’s brother, Mother’s brother, Father’s sister’s husband, or Mother’s sister’s husband |
| Niece      | Daughter of brother or sister                                             |
| Nephew     | Son of brother or sister                                                  |
| Cousin     | Child of aunt or uncle                                                    |
| In-Law     | Relationships formed by **marriage**, not birth                          |

## 🧩 Example Problems

## Example 1:
> *Introducing Neeta, Anil said, "She is the wife of my mother’s only son."*  
> **How is Neeta related to Anil?**

## 💡 Solution:
- Anil’s mother’s only son → Anil himself.
- Therefore, **Neeta is Anil’s wife**.

✅ Answer: **(2) Wife**

## Example 2:
> *Ram is the father of Kusha, but Kusha is not his son. Mala is the daughter of Kusha. Shalaka is the spouse of Ram. Gopal is the brother of Kusha. Hari is the son of Gopal. Meena is the spouse of Gopal. Ganpat is the father of Meena. Who is the granddaughter of Ram?*

## 💡 Solution:
- Kusha is **not** Ram's son → must be his **daughter**.
- Mala is the daughter of **Kusha** → Mala is the **granddaughter of Ram**.

✅ Answer: **(2) Mala**

## 🧾 7.3 Family/Blood Relations Described Generally

## Common Terms:
- **Parent**: Mother or Father
- **Child**: Son or Daughter (includes adults)
- **Sibling**: Brother or Sister (can be full or half siblings)
- **Spouse**: Husband or Wife

## Key Relationships:
- **Aunt**: Father’s sister, Mother’s sister, or spouse of Father’s or Mother’s brother
- **Uncle**: Father’s brother, Mother’s brother, or spouse of Father’s or Mother’s sister
- **Niece/Nephew**: Daughter/Son of your brother or sister
- **Cousin**: Child of your aunt or uncle

## 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 7.4 Relationships Involving '-in-law'

| Term             | Meaning                                      |
|------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| Father-in-law    | Father of your spouse                         |
| Mother-in-law    | Mother of your spouse                         |
| Son-in-law       | Husband of your daughter                      |
| Daughter-in-law  | Wife of your son                              |

> ❗ Note: These are **marriage-based** relationships, not blood-related.

## 👨‍👨‍👧‍👦 7.5 Father-in-law, Mother-in-law, Son-in-law & Daughter-in-law

| Term              | Description                                                                 |
|-------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Father-in-law     | Father of your spouse                                                       |
| Mother-in-law     | Mother of your spouse                                                       |
| Son-in-law        | Husband of your daughter                                                    |
| Daughter-in-law   | Wife of your son                                                            |

> ⚠️ If a spouse has children from a previous marriage, they are called **stepchildren**, not **sons-in-law** or **daughters-in-law**.

## 👨‍🤝‍👨‍👧‍👦 7.6 Brother-in-law & Sister-in-law

| Term               | Possible Meanings                                                                 |
|--------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Brother-in-law     | 1. Brother of your spouse<br>2. Husband of your sister<br>3. Husband of your spouse's sister |
| Sister-in-law      | 1. Sister of your spouse<br>2. Wife of your brother<br>3. Wife of your spouse's brother |

## 🕶️ 7.7 Half Sibling & Step Relations

| Term         | Description                                                                                     |
|--------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Half Sibling | Share one parent (either father or mother), not both                                         |
| Stepchild    | Child from a previous marriage of one parent (e.g., stepson or stepdaughter)                   |
| Stepparent   | New spouse of a parent after a divorce                                                        |

## 🧠 Self Assessment

1. If Ramesh says, "She is the daughter of my mother's only son," who is she?
2. What is the relationship between your brother's wife and your sister?
3. If A is the brother of B, and C is the father of A, what is B's relation to C?

## ✅ Answers for Self Assessment

1. She is Ramesh's **sister**.
2. Your **brother's wife** is your **sister-in-law**.
3. B is the **son** of C.
