## **Introduction**
Problems related to ages are commonly found in competitive exams. These problems typically involve:

1. **Age some years ago**
2. **Present age**
3. **Age some years hence**

These problems often require forming and solving linear equations. Additionally, shortcut methods can simplify calculations significantly.

## **Shortcut Methods**

## **Method 1**: Age Some Years Ago vs Present Age
If the age of A, `t` years ago, was `n1` times the age of B and currently A's age is `n2` times that of B:

- **A’s present age** = $\frac{n_2}{n_2 - n_1} \cdot t$
- **B’s present age** = $\frac{t}{n_2 - n_1}$

## **Method 2**: Future Age Relationship
If the present age of A is `n1` times the age of B and after `t` years, A's age will be `n2` times that of B:

- **A’s present age** = $\frac{n_2}{n_2 - n_1} \cdot t$
- **B’s present age** = $\frac{t}{n_2 - n_1}$

## **Method 3**: Mixed Past and Future Conditions
If A’s age `t1` years ago was `n1` times B’s age and after `t2` years, A’s age will be `n2` times that of B:

- **A’s present age** = $\frac{n_1 t_2 + n_2 t_1}{n_1 - n_2}$
- **B’s present age** = $\frac{(n_1 - 1)t_2 + (n_2 - 1)t_1}{n_1 - n_2}$

## **Method 4**: Sum of Present Ages
If the sum of present ages of A and B is `S`, and `t` years ago, A's age was `n` times that of B:

- **A’s present age** = $\frac{nS - (n - 1)t}{n + 1}$
- **B’s present age** = $\frac{S - (n - 1)t}{n + 1}$

## **Method 5**: Future Age Ratio
If the sum of present ages of A and B is `S`, and after `t` years, A’s age will be `n` times that of B:

- **A’s present age** = $\frac{nS - t(n - 1)}{n + 1}$
- **B’s present age** = $\frac{S - t(n - 1)}{n + 1}$

## **Method 6**: Age Ratios
If the ratio of present ages of A and B is `a:b` and after `t` years becomes `c:d`:

- **A’s present age** = $\frac{t(ad - bc)}{cd - ab}$
- **B’s present age** = $\frac{t(bd - ac)}{cd - ab}$

## **Examples & Solutions**

| Example | Description | Solution |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Example 1 | Father is 4 times his son's age; 5 years ago, father was 7 times son's age | Father's present age = $4 \cdot 5 = 20$ years |
| Example 2 | Mr Gupta is 4 times his son's age; after 10 years, he'll be twice as old | Son's present age = $10 / (4 - 2) = 5$ years |
| Example 3 | 10 years ago, Anu’s mother was 4 times older; after 10 years, she’ll be twice older | Anu's present age = $(10 \cdot (4 - 2)) / (4 - 2) = 20$ years |
| Example 4 | Sum of ages of A and B is 42; 3 years ago, A was 5 times B | A's present age = $ (5 \cdot 3 + 42) / (5 + 1) = 33$ years |
| Example 5 | Sum of ages of father and son is 56; after 4 years, father is 3 times son | Father's present age = $ (56 \cdot 4) / (3 + 1) = 44$ years |
| Example 6 | Ratio of father and son's ages is 6:1; after 5 years, it becomes 7:2 | Son's present age = $ (5 \cdot (7 - 6)) / (7 - 6) = 5$ years |
| Example 7 | 6 years ago, Mahesh was twice as old as Suresh; ratio of present ages is 9:5 | Difference = $ (5 \cdot 6) / (9 - 5) = 24$ years |
